## WAITLIST FORM STRUCTURE (build this before pushing more traffic)

Every signup must answer one question before submitting:

**"I am joining as a..."**
- Student
- Tutor or Teacher
- Consultant or Expert

This single field is what lets you follow up with three completely different messages later instead of one generic email to everyone. Do not skip this. A waitlist that cannot tell a student apart from a tutor is just a list of email addresses, not a usable asset.

Optional second field, shown only if "Student" is selected:
**"What are you preparing for?"**
- MDCAT
- ECAT
- O-Level / A-Level
- Other

This lets you segment further later if you ever want to message MDCAT-specific updates separately from ECAT-specific ones.

## Copy notes for whoever builds this page

Keep the student hero section visually dominant. Tutor and consultant sections should look like real sections, not a footnote, but the page should not open trying to speak to all three at once. Order on the page should follow the order above.

AI is mentioned once, in Section 6, placed deliberately after the core student pitch and the urgency section, not in the hero and not in the headline. This is intentional. A free competitor already offers generic AI mock tests and an AI tutor, so leading with "we have AI" would put EduConnect in a fight it cannot win on day one. The angle used here is personalization built from a student's real tutoring history on the platform, something a standalone AI app cannot replicate because it has no access to that student's actual sessions. Do not move this section higher or turn it into the hero headline. If AI needs to be mentioned again anywhere else on the page, keep it brief and keep the personalization framing, never a generic "AI powered" claim on its own.
